üüß Surface/Oxygen Tables (SOX15)
Normal Use SOX15
‚ñº
Tables with oxygen ("SOX" code) are for normal use
Surface decompression tables with air only ("SAB" code) are backup only for oxygen failure
If oxygen fails before planned dive: postpone dive until oxygen is available
Based on repetitive interval of 12 hours
Repetitive dives possible at intervals of not less than 2 or 4 hours using H2SOX15 or H4SOX15
After repetitive dive: observe interval of not less than 12 hours before next dive
In-water stops on air - surface stops on oxygen (20 min periods alternated with 5 min air)
SAB15 (backup air table) - use only if oxygen supply fails during dive
Do NOT plan dives using SAB15 as primary table
Emergency Procedures SOX15
‚ñº
Emergency procedures - follow carefully and consult diving supervisor
Oxygen Failure During Dive
If oxygen fails during in-water decompression: continue on air to surface
Do NOT attempt to complete planned oxygen stops
Once at surface: use emergency protocols and observe strict chamber confinement (4 hours minimum)
If oxygen fails before planned surface decompression: use SAB15 table as contingency
Delay in Surface Decompression
If delay occurs before reaching surface: extend in-water stops proportionally
If delay > 10 minutes: consult diving supervisor for modified decompression schedule
Always account for total bottom time when calculating surface decompression
Interruptions During Surface Decompression
If surface decompression interrupted for medical or operational reasons: reassess complete dive
Resume procedures according to conservative oxygen protocol (20 min on / 5 min off)

üö® Decompression Sickness (DCS) Recognition

Immediate Symptoms (Minutes to hours after dive):
Joint pain, muscle aches, tingling, weakness, fatigue, dizziness, nausea
Serious Symptoms (Minutes to hours):
Difficulty breathing, chest pain, paralysis, loss of consciousness, disorientation
ACTION: Call emergency services immediately - patient needs recompression therapy
Do not delay seeking treatment

üè• Emergency Response Protocol

1. Alert: Call emergency medical services / dive emergency number
2. Assessment: Identify symptoms and time of onset (critical for treatment)
3. First Aid: Administer 100% oxygen (if available) while awaiting emergency transport
4. Position: Keep patient horizontal, monitor vitals, prevent aspiration
5. Transport: Arrange immediate transport to nearest hyperbaric facility
6. Treatment: Recompression therapy in chamber under medical supervision (USN-5 or USN-6)
7. Follow-up: Medical evaluation and extended oxygen therapy as prescribed

‚ö†Ô∏è Oxygen Toxicity (OTu/VENTOX-C) Recognition

Early Warning Signs: Vision changes (tunnel vision), tinnitus, nausea, twitching, dizziness
Progressive Symptoms: Disorientation, muscle twitching, chest pain, difficulty breathing
ACTION: Immediately stop oxygen breathing, switch to air, ascend to shallower depth or surface

üÜò Surface Interval Problems

If symptoms appear during surface interval: Remove from further diving, monitor closely
If symptoms worsen: Contact medical facility, consider recompression therapy assessment
Next dive clearance: Medical evaluation required - never resume diving without medical clearance
